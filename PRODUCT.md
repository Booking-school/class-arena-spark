# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Scholar Hall serves Thai schools with several user groups in one system: public visitors who need to book rooms, students who join classrooms and complete learning activities, teachers who run classes and check work, and admins who manage rooms, users, approvals, and operations. Users are often scanning between many small tasks during the school day, so the product must feel calm, legible, and quick to act on.

## Product Purpose

Scholar Hall connects public room booking, classroom management, daily learning quests, AI tutoring, rewards, and admin oversight in one web app. Success means each role can land on the right surface, understand current priorities immediately, and finish common workflows without needing separate tools or manual coordination.

## Brand Personality

Focused, friendly, capable. The interface should feel modern enough for a digital classroom product, but not flashy or decorative. It should borrow the clarity and trust of education SaaS references such as Teachmint while keeping Scholar Hall's own identity and avoiding heavy, dark, or overly saturated classroom theming.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic gamified dashboards that drown learning tasks in badges and color. Avoid dark, dense enterprise admin screens that feel too heavy for schools. Avoid marketing-only landing pages that explain the product without giving users a direct route into booking, login, or classroom work. Avoid inconsistent controls, tiny touch targets, unlabeled inputs, and role flows that redirect users unexpectedly.

## Design Principles

1. Role first: public visitors, students, teachers, and admins should each see their highest-value work before secondary features.
2. Calm classroom energy: use gamification as feedback and motivation, not as visual noise.
3. Operational trust: admin and teacher screens should privilege clear labels, predictable controls, and visible state over decorative effects.
4. Accessible by default: every common action should work comfortably on desktop and mobile with clear labels, 44px touch targets, and readable contrast.
5. Public to private continuity: the landing page should introduce the same product vocabulary users meet after login.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for contrast, keyboard navigation, labeling, and focus visibility. The app should support Thai-first content with English where useful, preserve readable spacing for mixed Thai and English UI, respect reduced motion, and avoid relying on color alone for status.
